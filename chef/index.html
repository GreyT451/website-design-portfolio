01 — Problem

In today's fast-paced lifestyle, young city dwellers and busy professionals struggle to find the time, energy, and inspiration to cook at home — leaning on fast food, frozen meals, and takeout that compromise both health and culinary creativity.

Despite smart appliances and cooking apps, there's a lack of solutions that seamlessly blend convenience, personalization, and engagement to make home cooking effortless and enjoyable.

04 — Form & iterations

From sketch, to cardboard, to clay.

Dozens of silhouettes explored a single idea — a tablet that rests on a soft cylindrical roll, free to tilt to any angle. The form was pressure-tested in paper and foam before it was ever rendered.

04.1 — Design parameters

A design language, written as rules.

Six small-detail principles keep every surface, edge and seam unmistakably Chef — each one a clear do and don't.

D-01

Small details

Using highlights in the form of thin strips of light or a change of material.

Do · stripsDon't · clutter
D-02

Clean lines

Straight lines, 90-degree angles, minimal curved edges.

Do · straightDon't · curvy
D-03

Uniform radius

Corners are uniform, with a perfect, consistent radius throughout.

Do · uniformDon't · mixed
D-04

Geometric shapes

Following perfect geometry — regular rectangles, squares or circles. No ovals or parallelograms.

Do · regularDon't · oval
D-05

Colour & material break

A distinct line of separation marks any change in colour or material of the form.

Do · clear breakDon't · blend
D-06

Distinctive shapes

When two elements intersect, they blend while keeping their own identity.

Do · keep identityDon't · merge
